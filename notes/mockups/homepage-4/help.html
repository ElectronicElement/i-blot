How do I publish a post?

Move the file containing your blog post into Blot’s folder in your Dropbox. Blot will automatically publish it to your blog. Blot's folder is here – you can rename it if you wish.

What type of files can I use?

Blot can turn text files (.txt), markdown files (.md) and HTML files (.html) into blog posts. Blot will also convert image files (jpg, gif and png) into image posts automatically.

Tags

You can specify tags at the top of the entry’s file. The tags are case insensitive and can contain multiple words:

Tags: literature, book reviews, Stendhal

Pages

Adding a page is similar to publishing an entry — just create a text file with the page’s content then add this line at the very top of the file: 

Page: yes

{{name}} Your name ()
{{handle}} Your username ()

{{sitemapURL}} URL to your blog’s sitemap

You can specify a post’s date the top of its file. Blot supports plenty of date formats. 23/06/2012, 23.6.12 and 23-6-2012 all work just as well:

Date: June 23rd 2012

Can I schedule an entry to be published later?

Yes, you can specify the date to publish the entry at the top of the file. Blot will wait until that date to publish it to your blog. If you want to cancel the post, just remove the file from your folder.

Date: June, 13th 2020

How do I change an entry’s permalink?

You can specify a URL for blot to use at the top of the entry file.For example, to create a blog post with the URL .blot.im/cabbage put this at the top of your entry:

Permalink: cabbage

Can I restore a deleted entry?

Yes, just put the entry’s file back in Blot’s folder. Blot will automatically restore the entry, including its former URL. If you can’t find the file, check in Dropbox’s history.

Can I create folders in Blot’s folder?

Yes, absolutely. Blot doesn’t mind how you organize files inside its folder. I have created subfolders for each year.

What happens if my blog post is too large?

Blot ignores text files larger than 300kb (~75,000 words) in size. If one of your entries is larger than this, please consider splitting it into multiple entries for the sake of your readers. Please contact me if you need to lift this limit.
