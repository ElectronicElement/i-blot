Blot is a blogging platform. It creates a special folder in your Dropbox and publishes files you put inside automatically. You can use your favorite text‐editor to write your blog posts.

Why does Blot exist?

I like the flexibility and simplicity of plain text files. I've maintained a folder full of these files for years containing my notes. One day it occured to me that there's enough information encoded in the files and their metadata to generate a blog, without interfering with the structure of my folder, or the workflow for writing the files.

Why does Blot cost money?

The annual fee pays for the servers which host your blog. It also covers the cost of providing support. Most importantly, it encourages Blot to operate in your best interests, as opposed to those of an advertiser or investor.

Does Blot censor anything?

No, Blot does not censor anything that it can legally host.

Can I host Blot on my own server?

Yes, Blot’s source code is dedicated to the public domain.

How long has Blot been around?
