Menu and profile

You can change your site’s title, upload an avatar and edit the links on your site’s menu on the profile page.

Using your own domain

Let’s say you own the domain example.com and would like to point it to your site. First, you must decide whether you would like to use the apex domain, which is just example.com, or a subdomain like www.example.com or blog.example.com.

  • How to use a subdomain

    1. Go to your domain’s DNS page. This is usually where you bought the domain. Create a CNAME record which points to blot.im for the subdomain you’d like to use.

    2. Tell Blot your full domain, including the subdomain, on the dashboard.

  • How to use an apex domain

    1. Go to your domain’s DNS page. Create an ALIAS record which points to blot.im

    2. Tell Blot your domain on the dashboard.

If you cannot create an ALIAS record for your apex domain, use the www subdomain and redirect the apex domain.

Redirects

You can set up redirects for missing or moved pages on your blog. Your site’s 404 log will help you discover URLs which need redirects. You can use RegEx to match multiple routes. For example, this rule redirects URLs starting with /post to /blog:

\/post\/(.*)  →  /blog/$1
