Kleptography

I stole little bits here and there from all kinds of places to make Blot. Those burglaries are preserved here for future reference, just in case I ever dare think I had an original idea.


Pinboard

Stole much of the copy from their terms page and privacy policy. Macej’s design ‘philosophy’ was also influential. If I could only steal his sense of humour.


Kirby

Stole some of their marketing copy. Looking back, they’re likely the subliminal inspiration for Blot’s logo.


New York Times

Their type treatment of Georgia was ripped and used to design the default theme.


Cargo Collective

I think they do some really interesting, thoughtful work. I'm incredibly jealous, in fact. Stolen from their selection of themes to help make Blot’s.


Jekyll

A spiritual ancestor to Blot. A beautiful piece of software.


Stripe

Stole from their excellent support & documentation section when writing Blot’s documentation.


Subtraction

The original Blot template was basically lifted from Khoi Vinh’s blog.


Second Crack

The project which runs Marco Ament’s blog, shares a lot of the design principles of Blot. I didn’t see this until after opening Blot to the public and I wish I’d seen it sooner.


Notational Velocity

When I grow up I want to be like Zachary Schneirov.



I can't find good examples of individual things I've stolen but I really like the work of Tracy Ma, David Rudnick, Ben Barry, Jessica Svendsen, Rafaël Rozendaal, Peter Mendelsund.

As I often say: the bees pillage the flowers here and there but they make honey of them which is all their own.
