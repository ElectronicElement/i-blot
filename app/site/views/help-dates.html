Dates

The publish date is the file’s creation date by default. You can specify a different date in the file’s metadata or in its path. Blot will schedule a blog post for future publication if you specify a date that’s in the future. You can adjust your blog’s time zone and other date‐related settings on the dashboard.

Using metadata

The publish date is one of the properties of a blog post you can modify in the metadata at the start of a file. Here’s an example blog post with a modified publish date:

Date: {{#date}}MMMM Do, YYYY{{/date}}

Blot supports a variety of date formats. The following all produce a blog post with the same date:

{{#date}}MM/DD/YYYY{{/date}}   {{#date}}MMMM D, YYYY{{/date}}
{{#date}}M.D.YYYY{{/date}}     {{#date}}YYYY-MM-DD 00:00{{/date}}

Using the path

You can specify a date in a file’s name or its path. For example, in this blog's folder, all four posts share the same publish date:

2016
2017
{{#date}}YYYY{{/date}}
  {{#date}}M{{/date}} 
    {{#date}}D.[Post.txt]{{/date}}
  {{#date}}M-D.[Post.txt]{{/date}}
  {{#date}}M-D{{/date}}
    Post.txt
{{#date}}YYYY-MM-DD-00:00[-Post.txt]{{/date}}

The structure of the folder above is contrived but it illustrates a few ways to both keep your folder organized while specifying the date of your blog posts.
